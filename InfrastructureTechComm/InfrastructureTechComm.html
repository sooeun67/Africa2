Infrastructure - Technology & Communications

Across the 47 Sub-Saharan African Countries, 43% of their population has access to electricity. Burundi has the lowest level at 7.6%, while the Seychelles has highest level at 100%. Since 2000, levels have grown across all countries, though a wide disparity remains across the region.

Source: World Bank Indicator EG.ELC.ACCS.ZS
Created with: Python, seaborn
Though Internet Access has grown across all countries since 2000, disparity remains. In the average country, 20% of the population have access to the internet as of 2016. Seychelles, South Africa, Mauritius top the list.

Source: World Bank Indicator IT.NET.USER.ZS
Created with: Python, seaborn
Cellphone Subscriptions (a proxy for cellphone coverage in the country) are the highest in Seychelles, South Africa, Gabon, and Botswana. Eritrea is the lowest with just over 1 in 10 people with a cellphone subscription (10.21) in 2016. Similar to other indicators, though all countries have grown since 2000, disparity remains.

Source: World Bank Indicator IT.CEL.SETS.P2
Created with: Python, seaborn
Overall, there is a modest positive correlation (coefficient of 0.37) between a country’s growth rate of Internet Access over the past five years and its GDP Growth. Similar to Internet, there is a slightly positive correlation (coefficient of 0.29) between the growth rate of Cellphone Subscriptions over the past five years and GDP Growth. The trend appears to be stronger among those countries designated as 'low income'.

Source: World Bank Indicator IT.NET.USER.ZS, World Bank Indicator IT.CEL.SETS.P2
